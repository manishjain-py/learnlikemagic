# Principles: Chapter Prerequisites

These principles guide how we handle prerequisite knowledge gaps — the foundational concepts a student needs before a chapter will make sense. The goal is that every student arrives at Topic 1 actually ready to learn it, without ever feeling "behind."

## 1. Proactive, Not Reactive

Don't wait for a student to fail three questions before discovering they lack a foundation. Offer a prerequisite refresher *before* the chapter begins — as the first topic. A great tutor checks the basics before opening the textbook, not after the student is already confused and frustrated.

## 2. "Warm-Up," Never "Remediation"

Framing determines how a student feels about themselves. The refresher topic is a *warm-up* — something athletes and experts do — not a sign that the student is behind. The title says "Get Ready for [Chapter]," not "Missing Prerequisites." The content says "let's make sure you're set," not "you need to learn this first."

## 3. Just Enough, Not Comprehensive

The refresher teaches the *minimum* a student needs to follow this chapter — not a full lesson on each prerequisite. If the chapter needs place value for multiplication, teach "47 = 4 tens + 7 ones" — don't re-teach the entire place value topic. Think "Previously on Math..." recap, not a re-watch of the whole prior season.

## 4. Bridge to What's Coming

Every prerequisite concept should explicitly connect to the chapter ahead. Don't teach place value in isolation — teach it *as a building block for multiplication:* "We're reviewing this because when you multiply 24 x 3, you'll multiply the tens and ones separately." The student should feel the prerequisite is part of the journey, not a detour.

## 5. One Refresher Per Chapter

Keep it simple — one refresher topic per chapter, covering all critical prerequisites for that chapter. Don't create per-topic prerequisite checks; that fragments the experience and adds friction. A single well-crafted warm-up that covers the 3-5 foundations the chapter assumes is enough.

## 6. It's Just a Topic

The refresher is a regular topic — same explanation cards, same interactive teaching, same scorecard tracking. No special infrastructure. This means it gets the same quality bar (radical simplicity, multiple variants, review-and-refine passes) and the same student experience (cards, then interactive check). The student doesn't even know it's "different."

## 7. Optional, Not Gating

The refresher is always the first topic in the chapter, but students choose whether to do it. A confident student skips straight to Topic 1. A student who's unsure starts with the warm-up. Never force a student through prerequisites — some learn best by jumping in and filling gaps as they go. The existing in-session prerequisite detection (3+ wrong answers triggers a drill) remains as a safety net.

## 8. Cross-Boundary Awareness

Prerequisites often span chapters and grades. A Grade 3 multiplication chapter assumes Grade 2 place value. The refresher can cover concepts from anywhere — prior chapters, prior grades, even concepts the current book never explicitly teaches. It's self-contained; it doesn't require the student to have used the app for prior content.
