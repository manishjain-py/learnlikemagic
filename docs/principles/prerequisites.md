# Principles: Chapter Prerequisites

Guide prerequisite knowledge gaps so every student arrives at Topic 1 ready to learn, without feeling "behind."

## 1. Proactive, Not Reactive
Check basics *before* the chapter begins, not after 3 wrong answers.

## 2. "Warm-Up," Never "Remediation"
Title says "Get Ready for [Chapter]," not "Missing Prerequisites." Content says "let's make sure you're set," not "you need to learn this first."

## 3. Just Enough, Not Comprehensive
Teach the *minimum* needed for this chapter. "Previously on Math..." recap, not a full re-teach.

## 4. Bridge to What's Coming
Connect every prerequisite to the chapter: "We're reviewing this because when you multiply 24 x 3, you'll multiply the tens and ones separately."

## 5. One Refresher Per Chapter
One warm-up topic per chapter covering 3-5 foundations. No per-topic prerequisite checks.

## 6. It's a Topic (With Lighter Depth)
Same explanation cards, same tutor, same scorecard. But downstream systems (study plan, tutor prompts) recognize `is_refresher` and adapt: breadth over depth, quick checks not deep practice, 5-10 min not 20-40.

## 7. Optional, Not Gating
Students choose whether to do it. Never force prerequisites. In-session detection (3+ wrong answers) remains as safety net.

## 8. Cross-Boundary Awareness
Refresher can cover concepts from prior chapters, prior grades, or content the book never teaches. Self-contained.
