# Principles: Chapter Prerequisites

Guide prerequisite knowledge gaps so every student arrives at Topic 1 ready to learn, without feeling "behind."

## 1. Proactive, Not Reactive
Check basics *before* the chapter begins, not after 3 wrong answers.

## 2. Just Enough, Not Comprehensive
Teach the *minimum* needed for this chapter. "Previously on Math..." recap, not a full re-teach.

## 3. Bridge to What's Coming
Connect every prerequisite to the chapter: "We're reviewing this because when you multiply 24 x 3, you'll multiply the tens and ones separately."

## 4. One Refresher Per Chapter
One warm-up topic per chapter covering the critical foundations. No per-topic prerequisite checks.

## 6. No mastery tracking, no scoring
No mastery tracking, no scoring for prerequisites.

## 7. Optional, Not Gating
Students choose whether to do it. Never force prerequisites.

## 8. Cross-Boundary Awareness
Refresher covers ALL prerequisites the chapter assumes — from earlier chapters in the same book, prior grades, or concepts the book never teaches. Self-contained.
