# Core Principles

Guiding beliefs that shape every product and engineering decision in LearnLikeMagic.

---

## 1. The student is the only user that matters
Every feature, screen, and interaction exists to serve the K-12 learner. If a decision helps admins or looks impressive technically but doesn't improve the student's experience, it's the wrong decision.

## 2. Adapt to the learner, never the other way around
The tutor meets each student where they are — pace, language, interests, attention span, prior knowledge. The student should never have to adjust to the system. Personalization is not a feature; it is the product.

## 3. Teach like a great human tutor
Guide discovery instead of dispensing answers. Start simple, build progressively, vary approaches when something isn't working. Praise authentically, detect misconceptions, and know when to step back to fill prerequisite gaps.

## 4. Depth over breadth
One subtopic, one session, full understanding. Resist the urge to cover more ground at the cost of genuine comprehension. A student who deeply understands one concept is better off than one who has been superficially exposed to ten.

## 5. Effortless for kids
Every screen has one clear purpose. Minimal typing, large tap targets, friendly language, mobile-first. If a child can't figure out what to do in three seconds, the design has failed.

## 6. Respect the student's intelligence and time
Don't over-celebrate easy wins. Don't repeat what's already understood. Skip ahead when mastery is demonstrated. Let strong students move fast; give struggling students the time they need.

## 7. Curriculum-grounded, not free-form
All teaching is anchored in real textbook content and structured study plans. The AI is a tutor for a specific curriculum, not a general-purpose chatbot. This constraint is a strength — it keeps sessions focused and outcomes measurable.

## 8. Measure what matters
Track mastery and coverage, not just engagement. A session that leaves a student truly understanding fractions is more valuable than one that keeps them chatting for 30 minutes. Build evaluation into the system so tutor quality is continuously tested.

## 9. Ship simple, iterate fast
Prefer the simplest solution that works. Avoid premature abstraction and over-engineering. Get real student feedback, measure impact, and improve in tight loops.
