## Flow Assessment

The session is structurally top-heavy — 14 cards covering five steps, three check methods, two-part problems, and error-checking all before the student has tried a single problem. Cards 1-8 have solid logic and a consistent analogy, but Cards 9-14 pile on new complexity (subtraction, meta-level error checking, a dense summary) at the exact point where a struggling student most needs things to slow down. The card-to-interactive transition is warm and anchored well to the analogy, but it arrives after too much accumulated cognitive load for this student profile.

## Strengths

The packing-a-bag analogy is the session's clearest strength — it is introduced early, visually mapped to the steps, and reused consistently across multiple cards and into the transition question, giving the student a reliable mental anchor. The Indian names and contexts (Riya, Aman, mangoes, cricket) feel genuinely tailored rather than generic, and using 'Riya' as the character in Card 3 is a particularly warm touch for a student of that name.

## Flagged Messages

### 1. Turn 0 — unnatural_language [MINOR]

> Hi! Today we'll use **addition** to solve little story problems, like counting

Describes 14 content-heavy cards as 'some short cards' — a mismatch that sets a false expectation. A student told to expect 'some short cards' and then faced with 14 cards covering 5 steps, 3 check methods, two-part problems, and error-checking may feel misled or overwhelmed from the start.

*Context:* This is the only framing the student gets before 14 dense cards. Getting this wrong means the student enters the card sequence without an accurate sense of what's coming.

### 2. Turn 0 — information_dump [MAJOR]

> [Card 7] Now let's solve and check. Step 4 — Solve: 156 + 238. Remember regrouping

This card is teaching step 4 of the problem-solving process but buries a full regrouping walkthrough (three separate digit operations with carries) mid-explanation, doing two cognitive jobs at once. For a student who needs 2-3 passes per concept, combining these makes both harder to absorb.

*Context:* Card 6 cleanly set up the balloon problem and ended on a clear setup. Card 7 then recaps regrouping arithmetic mid-explanation of problem-solving steps. A student who is still processing the 5-step framework now also has to track digit-by-digit arithmetic inside the same message.
