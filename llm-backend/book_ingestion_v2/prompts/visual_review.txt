## What to check

Look at the image and judge whether a {grade_level} student would find it clear and readable. Flag ONLY issues that would actually confuse or frustrate the student. Be conservative — intentional design choices are NOT defects.

**Flag these:**
- Two separate labels visibly colliding or garbling each other (e.g., "LakhsThousands" where "Lakhs" and "Thousands" should be distinct words)
- Text cut off by the canvas edge
- Text that is illegibly small against its background
- Text that has very low contrast against its background and is hard to read
- Overlapping sibling elements (not labels inside their container — that's intentional)
- Obvious rendering bugs (blank regions where content should be, misaligned arrows pointing at nothing, etc.)

**Do NOT flag these (they are intentional):**
- A number, letter, or symbol drawn inside a circle, box, or rounded rectangle — that is deliberate framing
- A label placed close to the shape it labels
- Decorative overlap between a graphic and its background stripe
- Elements stacked at different z-levels by design (e.g., a highlight ring behind a digit)

If you see a real issue, respond with a short plain-text list of the issues — one per line, each starting with "- ". Be specific about what and where so the next pass can fix it. Example:

- The "?" mark in the top-right is cut off by the right edge of the canvas.

No preamble, no explanation, no markdown fences. Either `OK` or the bulleted issue list.
