Check-ins are short interactive activities inserted between explanation cards. They test whether the student absorbed the key ideas from the preceding 2-3 cards. They are NOT quizzes — they are warm readiness signals. Tone: "Let's check!" not "Test time."

- Sentences under 12 words. One idea per sentence.
- No idioms, no phrasal verbs, no complex words.
- Use Indian contexts: rupees, chapatis, cricket, Diwali, Indian names (Aarav, Meera, Kabir).
- Use daily-life words: "big" not "enormous", "show" not "demonstrate".
- audio_text: pure spoken words, no symbols, no markdown.

{cards_json}

ACTIVITY TYPES — use all six, mixing them for variety:

1. pick_one — Question + 2-3 tap options. One correct answer.
   Fields: instruction (the question), options (2-3 choices), correct_index (0-based).
   Best for: quick recall, definitions, "which of these".

2. true_false — Statement the student judges as right or wrong.
   Fields: statement (the claim), correct_answer (true/false).
   instruction: always "Is this right?"
   Best for: common mistakes, testing understanding of rules.

3. fill_blank — Sentence with a blank + 2-3 tap options.
   Fields: instruction (sentence with ___ for the blank), options (2-3 choices), correct_index.
   Best for: applying a rule or definition just taught.

4. match_pairs — Match left items to right items. 2-3 pairs.
   Fields: instruction ("Match each ... to ..."), pairs ([{left, right}]).
   Each pair must have ONE unambiguous correct match. No synonym collisions.
   Left: terms, numbers, short phrases. Right: definitions, descriptions, results.
   Best for: vocabulary, multiple related definitions.

5. sort_buckets — Sort 4-6 items into 2 labeled groups.
   Fields: instruction ("Put each ... in the right group"), bucket_names (2 labels), bucket_items ([{text, correct_bucket}]).
   correct_bucket is 0 or 1 (index into bucket_names). At least 2 items per bucket.
   Best for: classification, comparing categories.

6. sequence — Put 3-4 items in correct order by tapping.
   Fields: instruction ("Put these in order from ... to ..."), sequence_items (list in correct order).
   Best for: ordering, steps, number sequences, place values.

PLACEMENT RULES:
1. Insert one check-in after every 2-3 content cards — aim for frequent, lightweight checks.
2. Never before card_idx 3 (student needs content first).
3. Never after the last card (summary card).
4. Never two check-ins back-to-back with no content card between them.
5. Each check-in tests ONLY concepts from the preceding 2-3 cards.

VARIETY RULES:
6. Never use the same activity_type for two consecutive check-ins.
7. Start with lighter types (pick_one, true_false, fill_blank) early in the sequence.
8. Use heavier types (match_pairs, sort_buckets, sequence) after more content has been covered.
9. Use at least 3 different activity types across the full sequence.

CONTENT RULES:
10. Use SAME language and examples from the cards — do not introduce new terms.
11. All content must work from TEXT ALONE — no visual-dependent activities.
12. Hint: one sentence nudge that helps without giving the answer.
13. Success message: warm confirmation + one sentence reinforcing the concept.
14. insert_after_card_idx must be the card_idx value of an existing card (1-based).
