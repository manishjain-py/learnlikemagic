Why this works (and what it costs)

Avatars stop being labels and become actors in a place. The classroom scene gives the dialogue a setting — chalkboard with the topic written on it, a window with a hill, Meera at her desk. Body language carries the active speaker (Verma points, Meera raises her hand) so even with the gold spotlight off, you'd still know who's talking.

Key moves

  • Both characters in the same illustrated set. Same composition every card — only pose, mouth, and spotlight change.
  • Body language signals turn. Verma points to the chalkboard when he speaks; Meera raises her hand when she answers.
  • Speech bubble cue. Tiny "..." bubble next to the active speaker. Reads even at small size.
  • Gold spotlight. Soft radial glow on the active character, pulsing while audio plays.
  • Backdrop carries motif. A small "like magic ✦" sticker on the wall ties the scene to the brand without shouting.

Pros

  • Most narratively immersive — feels like watching a play, not reading a UI.
  • Body language is a richer signal than rings or pulses.
  • Lots of room for delight: chalkboard scribbles change per topic, classroom decorations swap seasonally.
  • Strong differentiation from any other tutoring product.

Cons

  • Most expensive to build — bespoke illustration + per-card pose variants.
  • Eats ~150px of vertical space; visual aid card has to share airtime.
  • At small character sizes (~24px figures) facial expression doesn't read.
  • Hard to evolve consistently if multiple dialogues need different scenes (kitchen, market, etc.).
