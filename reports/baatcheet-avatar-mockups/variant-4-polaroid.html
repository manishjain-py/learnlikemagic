Why this works (and what it costs)

This treatment leans hardest into the chalkboard metaphor. Polaroid + washi tape + handwriting cues a real teacher's classroom in a way no clean digital UI can — and avatars rendered with bokeh, soft skin gradient, and lens reflections feel like photographs rather than icons. Listener stays present as a small "pinned to the side" polaroid, so the relational dynamic survives.

Key moves

  • Polaroid frame. White paper border, washi tape, drop shadow, slight rotation. Reads as physical artifact.
  • Handwritten name. Caveat font on the white border — reinforces "teacher wrote this on the photo."
  • Listener as small corner polaroid. Smaller, more tilted, dimmed. Both characters always present, but the speaker dominates.
  • Photographic SVG. Skin gradients, bokeh background, lens reflections on glasses, hair highlights. Trying to feel "real" rather than "designed."
  • Glow on the speaker's polaroid. Subtle pulsing 2px gold border — like the photo is "lit" by the audio.

Pros

  • Strongest "real classroom" cue — physical objects beat digital pills.
  • Both characters present without taking too much space (secondary polaroid is 76px).
  • Handwritten captions add personality the chalk-gold pills lack.
  • Treatment is bespoke enough that it becomes brand language for Baatcheet.

Cons

  • Faking photographic SVG is risky — if it's not pulled off well it looks worse than honest illustration.
  • Lots of moving pieces (rotation, tape, shadows) = more rendering cost on low-end Android.
  • Polaroid metaphor may feel dated/twee to older students. Wins for grade 3-6, less for grade 7-8.
  • To do this for real, we'd want commissioned illustrations of the characters, not AI-style generic faces.
